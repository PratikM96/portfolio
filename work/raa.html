The situation

Davos in the Desert

The Future Investment Initiative is Saudi Arabia's flagship global investment forum, the gathering the press calls Davos in the Desert, and Richard Attias and Associates builds it end to end. For the 2019 edition at the Ritz-Carlton and the King Abdulaziz Conference Center, one visual system had to run across every surface at once: the hall, the screens, the printed program, the signage, the app, and the broadcast feed, bilingual in English and Arabic, in front of heads of state.

01 / The visual system

Twelve themes, one grid

As Associate Designer I worked the environmental, digital, and print creative across the edition, all of it holding to one bilingual identity, a dark, premium base with a luminous teal-to-green network motif running from the printed factsheet to the screens to the hall. The 2019 program was built around three pillars, three summits, and twelve cross-sector taskforces, each with a one-word codename. Twelve distinct ideas, one grid that still reads as a single forum.

The FII 2019 factsheet and program collateral, the constellation identity holding twelve taskforces on one grid. Imagery from the official FII 2019 factsheet produced by Richard Attias and Associates.

02 / Environmental

Environmental design is UX for physical space

The same identity carried stage and main-hall scenic built to broadcast-safe zones, large-format installs and bilingual wayfinding that moved attendees through the venue, digital signage, session displays, and the full suite of printed delegate materials. It also carried the launch of the FII Institute during the edition, dense institutional content that had to stay readable and credible for sovereign-fund chiefs and global press. Print, screen, and hall, all speaking one language at once.

The result

Every surface delivered on schedule, on brand, and on camera. FII 2019 was one of the most visible business gatherings in the world that year, and the creative had to be invisible in the right way, so coherent that the system, not any single asset, was what people remembered.

Sub-project / Global AI Summit

Two arguments about AI, one brief

Alongside FII I explored the identity for the Global AI Summit, a related forum positioning the Kingdom in the AI conversation. It was an exploration that did not go to production, but the thinking is the point: a bilingual system, Futura for English and Montserrat Arabic for Arabic, with two logo directions on the Ai letterform. One reads the A as a faceted, machine-built form in a warm orange-to-red gradient, data made visible. The other reads the Ai as a figure mid-stride in a cool green-to-teal, the human at the center of the AI story. Two directions, two arguments about what the summit really was.

The Global AI Summit exploration, two logo directions and the bilingual brand spec, palette and typography in one system.

What it taught me

FII is the headline, but the real education was the standard. The agency builds these forums end to end, and my time there ran across several, FII, Bloomberg's New Economy Forum, and the pitch work that wins the next one. Holding world-class the second a head of state walks in, in two languages, set the bar I work to now.
