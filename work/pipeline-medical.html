Pipeline Medical · UI/UX / Brand

An intern built it in 2017.
It is still running.

I built the brand and the marketplace for Pipeline Medical as a 2017 intern, for procurement buyers with zero tolerance for ambiguity. It is live today, expanded far past the three screens we shipped. Good structure outlives its brief, and this is the proof.

2017 · Designer
The situation

No tolerance for ambiguity

Pipeline Medical needed a place where healthcare procurement teams could browse, evaluate, and buy supplies and capital equipment online. There was no brand, no digital presence, and no channel to reach buyers. It had to read credible and clear for an audience that punishes ambiguity in a purchasing decision, where a moment of doubt is a lost sale.

01 / Brand identity

Clinical, credible, clean

A logo system for digital and print, a horizontal lockup, a vertical variant, and full-color versions, replacing a dated wave mark with a clean modern identity. Clinical and credible, which is the right register for a B2B medical supplier whose buyers are procurement teams, not consumers.

The rebrand, from the original wave mark to the system it became.
02 / Marketplace UX

A procurement workflow, not a website

The marketplace ran across three screens, a landing page, a product catalog, and a cart, all designed around how procurement actually buys, with clear categories, a browsable catalog, and a flow built for bulk and repeat orders. Designed in Adobe XD and built on Shopify, with Google display aimed at medical-equipment and injectable-supply buyers running around it.

Three screens, the landing page, the catalog, and the cart, built around how procurement buys.
03 / Ads, collateral & social

Everything the launch needed

Google display ads by product category, buyer-education one-pagers, the Capital Equipment List the procurement team worked from, and a brand social presence rounded out the launch, every piece held to the same clinical system.

Buyer-education one-pagers, what Pipeline does and how it helps a procurement team.
Display ads, built by product category for medical-supply and injectable buyers.
The brand system carried into the email and social headers.
Built to outlast the brief

The platform launched at supplies.pipelinemedical.com and is still live, expanded into pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and GLP-1 medications, with distribution partners including Allergan, 3M, Molnlycke, and Provista. It kept growing well past the original scope, the proof of the foundation the first build set.

What it taught me

Pipeline was my early proof of the payoff in building to last. Procurement buyers do not forgive a moment of doubt, so every screen had to earn trust before it asked for a decision, and building it that way is why the platform is still live and scaling well past what we shipped in 2017. Designing for the foundation instead of the launch is why the platform outlived its brief, the difference between a brand lead and a project designer.
