Speculative Is the New Portfolio

The Ninth · WISP · LevelConcept · brand systems · AIMay 24, 20266 min read

Alongside paid work, I build complete brand systems for companies that don't exist. An esports membership product, an AI host with no face, a prediction market that protects its users. No client briefed them, no budget approved them. I build them because speculative work has quietly become one of the most useful things a creative director can do, especially now.

It proves range a case study cannot

Client work is bounded by what the client needed, the budget, and the committee. A speculative concept has none of those ceilings. It lets me design a full system end to end: brand, product UI, motion, social, and a working AI layer, and to show the cross-channel thinking with nothing watered down in approvals. For someone hiring a creative leader, a concept built whole is often a clearer signal of how a person thinks than a real project shaped by constraints they'll never see.

It's how I take a position on where things are going

Each concept starts from an industry that's large now or rising fast, and treats AI two ways on purpose: a functional AI layer the user actually touches, and an AI-accelerated pipeline that builds the work. That's not decoration. It's a way to think hard about the near future before a client asks me to. By the time the brief arrives, I've already designed for the world it lives in. Speculative work is how I stay ahead of the curve instead of reacting to it.

A client project shows what I did. A speculative system shows what I would do if nobody told me where to stop.

The constraint you keep is honesty

Speculative doesn't mean fictional in the dishonest sense. Every market figure in these concepts is sourced and describes the real industry, never a result I'm claiming. Projected outcomes are labeled as objectives, not wins. The discipline is to design ambitiously and stay rigorous about what's real, because the moment a concept invents its own success it stops being a credible argument and becomes a mood board.

Why this matters more in the AI era

AI is making it cheaper to produce a full speculative build, which means the bar is no longer whether you can make it look real. It's whether the thinking holds. The concepts that matter are the ones with a genuine point of view about a hard problem, designed all the way through. In a field where execution is getting commoditized, building something complete and opinionated that nobody asked for is one of the strongest things you can put in front of the world.

FAQ

Isn't speculative work just stuff that was never good enough for a real client?+
It's the opposite. A speculative system has no committee, no budget cap, and no scope cuts, so it shows the full range of how I think, end to end. Real projects are shaped by constraints a viewer never sees, a concept isn't.
How is it honest if the company doesn't exist?+
Because the work never claims a result it didn't earn. Every market figure is sourced to the real industry, and any projected outcome is labeled an objective, not a win. The ambition is in the design, not in invented numbers.
Doesn't cheap AI production make speculative work less impressive?+
It makes looking real worthless and thinking clearly the whole game. When anyone can render a polished build, the only thing that separates concepts is whether they have a genuine point of view about a hard problem. That part AI can't fake for you.
