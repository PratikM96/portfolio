Audience-First, Org-Second: The Business Goal Is the Destination, the Audience Is the Road

DealNews · SPORTIMEBrand · product · methodApril 4, 20265 min read

Most brand and product work starts in the wrong place. It starts with the organization, the goal, the launch date, the thing the company needs people to do, and the audience gets bolted on later as a target to hit rather than a person to understand. I have never run a project where the right answer started with the org instead of the person. Reverse the order and the hard calls get easier, because you stop guessing about the one variable you can actually go and check.

Start with what the person already knows

The first artifact on any project I run is not a logo or a wireframe, it is one page describing the exact mental state the person arrives in. Not a persona with a stock photo and a fake name, the real thing, what they already know, what they fear, what they need to see in the first few seconds to keep going. An esports fan does not want another hoodie, they want a reason to show up on a dead Tuesday with no match on the calendar. A first-time prediction-market user does not want a trading terminal, they want to read a probability without feeling hustled. Get that page right and the brief writes half of itself, because most design arguments are really arguments about who the work is for, and you have already settled that.

The DealNews rebuild made the cost of skipping that page obvious. The app served 17M-plus monthly users, and the wins in 2021 did not come from a prettier interface, they came from designing around what a deal-hunter is doing in the three seconds before they bounce, which is scanning for a price and a reason to trust it. We cut everything that did not serve those three seconds. On-site conversions rose 18 percent and both store ratings climbed, Google Play from 3.8 to 4.6 and the App Store from 4.3 to 4.7. Those numbers were the audience telling us, in the only language that counts, that we had finally understood them.

The org's goals get met on the way, not at the start

Audience-first does not mean ignoring the business, it means the business goal is the destination and the audience is the road you take to reach it. That distinction is the part that makes executives nervous, so I am precise about it in the room. At SPORTIME the company needed the new pickleball vertical to fill more than 200 Northeast courts in 2023, and the slow way to do that was to talk about the courts. The fast way was to build a place a specific kind of player wanted to belong to, a forty-something looking for a Saturday game and a few familiar faces, and let the bookings follow the belonging. Pickleball-specific inquiries rose by roughly 20 percent in the first six months, not because we sold courts harder but because we stopped selling courts and started speaking to a person.

Lead with the org and you get work that explains itself. Lead with the audience and you get work that earns attention.

It is a forcing function, not a philosophy

The reason to lead with the audience is not that it is virtuous, it is that it kills bad ideas earlier than any other test I know. Every feature, every headline, every animation faces the same single question: does this serve what the person actually knows and responds to, or does it serve our own comfort inside the building? Internal-comfort decisions are easy to spot once you are looking for them. The jargon nobody outside the office would ever say out loud. The feature on the roadmap because a senior stakeholder asked for it in a meeting eighteen months ago. The launch sequence built around the company's quarter instead of the moment the customer is actually ready to act. On the DealNews rebuild this settled a recurring fight over homepage real estate, where every team wanted its own section above the fold, by asking one question instead: does a deal-hunter scanning for a price need this in the first screen, or do we just want it there. Most of the contested blocks did not survive that question, and the ones that did had earned it. Audience-first turns those from taste debates, which nobody wins, into a yes-or-no check against a person you have already described, which is the difference between a meeting that ends and a meeting that loops.

Why this matters more now

When the tools can generate a thousand on-brief options in a minute, knowing which one is right for a specific person becomes the entire job. Gen AI is a production layer in my work, and it is genuinely good at volume, it will hand me a hundred competent directions before lunch. What it cannot do is tell me which of them a tired parent at 9pm, phone in one hand, half-watching something, will actually trust enough to tap. That call is not a feeling, it is the output of having described that parent honestly before anything got made. So the audience page is not a soft, nice-to-have step at the front of the process, it is the systems decision the whole project compounds on, the input that makes everything downstream cheaper to produce and sharper when it lands. The output got cheap. Knowing who it is for is the part that did not.

FAQ

Where does the real mental state actually come from, if not a persona doc?+
Three cheap sources before any expensive research: the words real users already use in reviews, support tickets, and search queries; one hour watching someone use the current thing without helping them; and the sales or front-desk team, who hear the actual objections daily. The mental state is usually already written down somewhere in the company. Most teams just have not read it back.
Does this hold when the audience is a business, not a consumer?+
It holds harder. A procurement lead or a CFO arrives with a sharper, narrower mental state than a consumer, less patience, more to lose, a specific number they are accountable for. Audience-first on a B2B buyer means leading with the risk you remove and the time you save, in their words, not with your product tour.
What do you do when a stakeholder insists on org-first?+
Agree on the destination out loud, then argue only about the route. Most org-first instincts are really a fear that the business goal will get lost. Name the goal explicitly, show how the audience-first route reaches it faster, and the disagreement usually dissolves, because it was never really about the order, it was about trust that the number still gets hit.
