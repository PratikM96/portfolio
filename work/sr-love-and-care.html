00 / Situation

The bottleneck was the org chart

I measure a role by what keeps running after I leave. Four years turning a five-person volunteer crew into a 15+ creative division for a global humanitarian nonprofit, and then the part that actually counts: handing it off as its own function.

The US division had no design team of its own. Every creative request funneled through the broader marketing function, a bottleneck for an organization running an expanding set of humanitarian programs across food relief, healthcare, and education. The fix was never going to be more output from one person. It was a team.

The brand outro animation, one half of the bookend system we built for the org's video content.
01 / The work

A team built to run without me

We founded a standalone volunteer design division from scratch and grew it from 5 to 15+ creatives over four years. I set the workflows, the briefing process, and the quality standards that let the team handle every collateral request on its own, separate from marketing, and I mentored the junior designers as it grew. The goal from day one was a team that did not need me in the room.

Across those years it produced brand animation, social graphics, brochures, posters, newsletters, and recap and testimonial videos.

A team built to run without me. That was the deliverable.

The brief was never really the brochures. It was a creative function that could run without me, and growing it from five volunteers to fifteen-plus meant designing workflows, standards, and people, not just artwork. Scaling a team and handing off my own judgment is the part of this that reads as leadership, not craft, and it is the experience I lean on hardest when I make the case for running a department, not just a project.
