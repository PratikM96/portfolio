Audience first. Built to run again.

I was supposed to be a programmer. I grew up in coding camps, studied computer science at NYU, and assumed I'd follow my dad into code. Then in 2016 I joined a nonprofit's marketing and design team to help build out its US division, and the plan changed. For the first time I was making things people actually responded to, not just things that ran, and I didn't want to go back. A couple of internships, an agency, and a run of in-house and startup roles took me further in.

The engineering brain never left. It just aimed at a different problem.

The audience is the system

I still think in systems first, but the system I care about most is the person on the other end. Before anything gets designed or built, I want to know what they already know, what they need, and what actually moves them. Everything after that is execution, and you can usually tell when work was built the other way around.

One idea, all the way through

I never picked a lane. Brand, motion, UI/UX, social, paid, I'd rather take one idea all the way through than hand it off five times and watch it lose the thread. If there's a throughline, it's motion. I think video is where a brand should start, not what it upgrades to.

Build it once

I'm wired to set things up right the first time, because anything I'll do more than once should cost less effort the next time, not the same again. That instinct is most of how I work. I'd rather spend the time up front on the system, the template, the process, so the next campaign or launch runs cleaner and the team can keep it going without me in the room.

I measure the work by what keeps running after I step back.

Off the clock

The other half of me is endlessly, uselessly curious, ancient history, architecture, cars, space, anime, coffee, whatever's in reach, to the point that friends have stopped asking why I know any of it. The one creative thing I haven't built yet is music. I've been singing since I was a kid, and an album is the long game, the project I'm quietly saving all this range for.
