SPORTIME didn't need more posts. It needed the machine that makes them. A video-first engine across three sub-brands and 15+ accounts, built so club teams could run it, with the reporting to prove it worked.

One system, five marks: SPORTIME Clubs, JMTA, JMTP, SPORTIME Pickleball, and SPORTIME VBC, built to hold together at every size.

The Situation

SPORTIME runs the largest tennis club network in the Northeast. By early 2023 it had three distinct verticals, tennis clubs, a new pickleball division, and the John McEnroe Tennis Academy, each needing its own identity while staying coherent inside the parent system.

The social presence was stagnant. Accounts across 15+ locations were barely growing, posting inconsistently, and underperforming for a network that size. At the same time, a $75M facility expansion across two JMTA campuses needed a complete marketing program ready before opening day.

One person. All of it.

The Work

Brand System

Built a complete logo production system covering five brand marks: SPORTIME Clubs, JMTA, SPORTIME Pickleball, Volleyball, and individual identities for all club locations. Each delivered in full source AI files with exports across every required format and colorway, plus a brand guideline template covering usage, spacing, and approved applications.

SPORTIME brand header motion, the system in movement.

SPORTIME Tennis / Social and Content Rebuild

Rebuilt the content strategy across all 13 club locations from the ground up. A video-first approach replaced static-heavy feeds. A unified marketing calendar coordinated output across every location. Event photography and a templated collateral system gave staff the tools to execute locally without the work going off-brand.

Result

From a near-zero posting baseline to 7,354 Instagram posts by early 2024, 3,115 of them Reels. Facebook organic impressions across the network rose 83% to 2.4M.

JMTA / John McEnroe Tennis Academy

The $75M expansion across Long Island and New York City campuses added courts, pickleball facilities, and a Community Welcome Center, ultimately making SPORTIME Randall's Island the largest indoor tennis training facility in the world at completion. All of that new capacity needed marketing materials ready before opening day, and enrollment momentum from day one.

Produced a full suite of print and digital assets across both campuses: event graphics, Team Cup promotional materials, seasonal campaign visuals, and co-branded SPORTIME and JMTA collateral, delivered simultaneously across print vendors, social platforms, and internal communications.

Result

All materials delivered ahead of opening. JMTA became the highest-reach account in the network within the year, the largest Instagram account at 15,154 followers (+3,131 for the year) and the top page on both platforms by organic impressions. The new facility reached approximately 65% court utilization within 90 days of opening.

SPORTIME Pickleball / Brand Identity and Launch

Pickleball grew 30% nationally in 2022 and was named the fastest-growing sport in America for the third consecutive year in 2023. SPORTIME needed a brand for its dedicated vertical that could stand alone, scale across 200+ courts, and support tournaments, clinics, and co-branded programs without requiring a designer at every location.

Developed a modernized logo system with cleaner typography and a refreshed badge format. Built a scalable template library covering tournament flyers, gift cards, open-play promotions, and event series graphics. Motion trailers and compilation reels produced to support launch and ongoing promotion.

Result

Vertical launched across 200+ Northeast courts with a full brand and content system in place. Membership inquiries for pickleball-specific programming increased approximately 20% in the six months following the rebrand, supported by a credible brand identity that competitors in the region had not established yet.

SPORTIME VBC / Volleyball

Alongside the three verticals, SPORTIME's volleyball club ran with almost no produced collateral. The channel was built on event photography: tournament coverage, team moments, and match-day energy shot, edited, and posted fast enough to ride the weekend.

Result

The VBC account punched above its size: first in the network by total organic engagements at 45,499 and second by organic impressions, despite a smaller following.

What it taught me

SPORTIME taught me to stop designing posts and start designing the engine that makes them. Members didn't show up for a polished one-off; they showed up for content that was consistent, fast, and recognizably theirs, and building that system is what moved the numbers. Owning a channel end to end, from strategy to production to the reporting that proves it worked, is the operator's view of the job, not the maker's. That end-to-end ownership is exactly what I want to lead at a larger scale.
