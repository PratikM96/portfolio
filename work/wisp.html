The premise

Every AI persona on the market is solving the wrong problem. The realistic ones spend their whole budget hiding that they are synthetic. The avatar ones borrow a crowded visual language. WISP does neither. It has no face, so there is nothing to fake and no uncanny valley to fall into. The form itself is the honesty.

An audience that no longer needs its AI to cosplay as human

The problem

Two failures, and a cliff

The uncanny valley

Human-passing AI personas spend their design budget hiding that they are synthetic. The result reads as slightly wrong, and now sits on the wrong side of disclosure law.

The crowded avatar

The VTuber lane borrows an established anime visual language, so each new character competes in a field of near-identical ones. Recognition is hard to earn.

The legal cliff

New York's synthetic-performer disclosure statute takes effect June 9, 2026, the FTC is enforcing AI-content disclosure, and 46 states have deepfake laws. Personas built to pass as human are accumulating trust and legal liability by the month.

The insight

Make honesty the design, not a disclaimer

Give the host no human or character form at all. A presence: light, motion, and voice. The moment it has no face, the uncanny valley is gone and there is nothing to fake. The form becomes the disclosure, so the brand is trustworthy and, as the new rules land, compliant by construction. Every hour of design then goes to the one thing nobody else is building: a non-human presence that is emotionally legible and genuinely good company.

Disclosure-native by design
Audience first

Built for the co-watch generation

The primary viewer

Late teens to late twenties who keep a stream running while they work, game, or scroll. They want ambient company with a personality, not a relationship that pretends to be real.

The AI-curious and the brands

People who find human-passing AI a little creepy and anime not their thing, plus brands that want reach with zero reputation or legal risk. The abstract form is the on-ramp for both.

Identity

Core plus field

A stable, recognizable core wrapped in a reactive field. The core is the constant you recognize in any frame. The field is the expression, always moving with voice and feeling. One continuous living material, never a sphere with effects bolted on. At rest, it breathes.

Palette

Deliberately warm, to avoid the cyan-on-black AI cliche. Color is a state channel, governed rather than decorative, with one reserved cool accent that always means something.

The core idea

Feeling without a face

The hardest, most ownable problem in the project. With no eyes or mouth, emotion is carried entirely by speed, brightness, color temperature, and how the form gathers or scatters. The brain emits one of seven states each turn, and that single value drives both the voice and the visible form. These swatches are live.

Idle

Soft, warm, slow breath.

Listening

Draws inward, leans in.

Thinking

Fragments, accent flickers.

Speaking

Articulates to voice.

Delight

Blooms, sparks.

Hesitation

Thins, wavers.

Quiet

Dims, a held breath.

How it works

One decision drives the voice and the form

WISP runs a loop fast enough to feel like it is reacting to you, not replying. The brain does not just return words. It returns a feeling, and that single value drives the voice and the visible form at once. Built on the proven real-time stack: a language model, a 75ms-class streaming voice, and an audio-reactive engine.

The business

How a presence earns

Content and community

Ambient co-watch streams build the daily habit, clips drive discovery, and a membership tier sells perks a presence can credibly give. The audience are the wisps.

Brand partnerships and IP

Reach with zero scandal risk and disclosure-safe integration, since the host is visibly synthetic and never fakes a human endorsement. Merch is the light made physical, not a mascot.

Objectives

What success looks like

A concept, so these are goals, not results. No outcome here is claimed as achieved.

  • The thirty-second test: a stranger watching a short clip understands, without being told, that this is not a person, that it is reacting live, and that it is somehow likeable.
  • A form recognizable enough to be proprietary IP from the first frame.
  • Emotional legibility a viewer can read across a room, learned unconsciously within minutes.
  • A trust and disclosure posture that is an advantage to brand partners rather than a liability.
Expected results

The metrics that would prove it works

Speculative, so these are the indicators the host would be measured on, with current industry benchmarks as the reference line. No figure here is claimed as achieved.

Reach and growth

Concurrent viewers, hours watched, follower growth, clip reach and impressions.

Engagement and retention

Engagement rate, average watch time, viewer retention, chat messages per minute, returning-viewer rate.

Reference: 40 to 60% retention is strong, AI personas index near 8.7% engagement against 4.5% for humans.

Presence quality

Response latency, uptime, moderation incident rate, chat sentiment, and state-fit, whether the form matches the moment.

Target: a sub-2-second loop, since lower latency correlates with higher live engagement.

Community and revenue

Membership trial-to-paid conversion, monthly churn, membership revenue, revenue per active viewer.

Reference: 45 to 65% trial-to-paid is strong, healthy churn sits at 2 to 5% monthly.

Brand-partner outcomes

Video completion, brand lift, ROAS and CPA, and disclosure compliance.

Reference: clips under 15s see ~23% higher completion, disclosure compliance is 100% by design.

Where it works

Where an honest presence fits

The same host has a clear set of viable contexts, and a few it should stay out of. Knowing the difference is part of the brand.

Always-on co-watch channel

Ambient company that never tires, awake whenever the audience is. The core use, and the daily-habit engine.

Brand activations

Sponsorships and takeovers with reach and zero reputation or legal risk, since the host is visibly synthetic.

Live launches and events

A tireless host for product drops, keynotes, and 24-hour streams, the format that spikes attention.

Second-screen companion

A reactive presence alongside sports, esports, and other streams, clipping and commenting in real time.

Retail and experiential

An audio-reactive presence in physical space, where the form and a venue's visuals become one instrument.

Global and multilingual

One identity, many languages, the same form and voice signature carrying across markets.

Where it should not go

Not a therapist or a medical or legal authority, not a stand-in for a human relationship, and never impersonating a real person. The boundaries are the brand, and naming them is a feature, not a hedge.

Approach

AI in the product, and in the making

Two layers, kept separate. In the product, AI is the live system: the language model, the voice, and the audio-reactive form. In production, generative tools accelerated the build, image models for styleframes, video models for motion tests, an LLM to stress-test the voice. The case study keeps that line clear, because pretending otherwise would be off-brand for a project about honesty.

The package

Concepted end to end

WISP was developed as a complete concept, not a mood board: strategy, persona, a testable brain, system architecture, go-to-market, a launch film, a visual brand guideline, and a working interactive demo.
