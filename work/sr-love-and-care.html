Disciplines
Brand / Campaign

Built a volunteer design division from 5 people to a fully independent 15-person creative team for a global humanitarian nonprofit, then handed it off as its own function.

Brand outro animation, part of the bookend system built for the org's video content.

The Situation

The US division had no dedicated design team. Every creative request ran through the broader marketing function, creating a bottleneck that slowed output for an organization running an expanding portfolio of global humanitarian initiatives across food relief, healthcare, and education.

The Work

Founded a standalone volunteer design division from scratch, growing it from 5 to 15+ volunteer creatives over four years. Established agile workflows, briefing processes, and quality standards that let the team handle all collateral requests independently from marketing. Mentored junior designers throughout.

Deliverables produced during this period include brand animation sequences, social media graphics, brochures, posters, newsletters, and video editing for recap and testimonial content.

A team built to run without me. The real deliverable.
Brand intro animation, the other half of the bookend system.

Campaigns & Social

Awareness campaigns, a four-panel GPS carousel, COVID relief drives, and seasonal posts produced across the team's run.

Print & Newsletter

Brochures, event banners, and newsletters for the organization's humanitarian programs.

Newsletter, top of the full multi-section spread.
August event recap, edited from live footage.
Result

By 2018 the team had grown large enough to formally separate from the marketing division as its own dedicated creative function, a 3x growth from founding size, built on infrastructure designed to sustain long-term output independently.
