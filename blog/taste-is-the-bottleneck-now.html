Taste Is the Bottleneck Now: When Anything Can Be Made, Judgment Is the Job

Creative directionJudgment · the CD roleMay 19, 20265 min read

For most of this field's history the constraint was production. Making the thing was hard, slow, and expensive, so the people who could make it well were valuable for exactly that. The tools have quietly removed the constraint. When anything can be generated in seconds, the bottleneck moves to the one thing that was never about production at all: knowing which of the thousand possible things is the right one to make, for this audience, on this problem. That is the whole job now, and it is a harder one than rendering ever was.

Making was the moat, and the moat is draining.

Be honest about what is happening to the craft, because pretending otherwise helps no one. A large share of what creative work used to charge for was execution, the hours and the skill of turning a rough idea into a polished asset, and Gen AI is collapsing that cost toward zero. On the SPORTIME video engine in 2023 I watched a process that should have taken a small team a week compress into something one person could run in a day, which is how a single year produced more than 7,000 assets across the network. The thing nobody quite says out loud is that the polish itself, the part that used to take the most hours and command the most respect, is now the cheapest part of the whole pipeline. That is genuinely destabilizing if execution was your whole value, and it is liberating if it was not, because the part getting expensive by comparison is judgment, and judgment was always the harder thing to find and the slower thing to build.

When everything is cheap, a director's job is subtraction.

When I can generate fifty directions in an afternoon, my job is not to make one, it is to throw away forty-nine for reasons I can defend out loud. It is knowing that this option will land with a tired parent scrolling at nine at night and that one will slide past unseen, and holding a single coherent intent across every surface so the fifty fragments read as one thing instead of fifty. The model makes the options, the job is the choice. Cheap generation actually makes this harder, not easier, because abundance is its own trap, and a team drowning in plausible options will ship the seventh-best one simply because choosing got overwhelming and the deadline did not move. I have sat in the review where fifty good directions were on the wall and the real work, the only work that mattered that day, was the argument for why one of them was right and the other forty-nine were not. None of that comes from a prompt, and the more output the tools produce, the more the scarce act becomes selection, direction, and the restraint to stop, which is the exact opposite of what cheap production quietly tempts you toward.

The tools can make anything. They can't tell you what's worth making. That gap is the whole job now.

Taste is not vibes, it is compressed experience.

Taste sounds soft, so people discount it, and that is a mistake. It is pattern recognition built from years of watching what worked, for whom, and why, then applied fast and under real pressure with a number on the line. A model has read everything and decided nothing. A director has decided a thousand times and remembers what happened the morning after each one, the campaign that tested beautifully and died in the feed, the safe option that quietly outperformed the bold one. The difference between reading about a thousand outcomes and living through them is the difference between knowing the rules of a game and having lost money playing it, and only one of those two teaches you what to actually do at the table. That accumulated judgment is exactly the thing that does not transfer into a model, because it lives in having been accountable for outcomes, not in having merely seen the examples, and accountability is not a dataset you can train on.

This is good news, and it raises the bar on you.

This is reassuring for anyone who got into the work to think rather than only to render, and it makes a real demand in the same breath. The role gets more strategic and less about labor, which is where creative direction should have been all along, closer to the brief and further from the export queue. The demand is that your judgment now has to be genuinely good, not just confidently asserted, and you have to be able to say why one option beats another in terms a client and an engineer both understand. That last part matters more than it sounds, because a director who cannot articulate the reason behind a choice used to be able to hide behind craft, and that hiding place is gone now that anyone can produce the craft. Taste becomes the deliverable, the thing they are actually paying for, and a deliverable you can be wrong about in public is a harder, better job than a polished file ever was.

FAQ

How do you actually build taste, rather than just claiming you have it?+
Reps with accountability, which is the part you cannot speed-run. You have to ship real work to real audiences, watch what happened, and own the misses, because taste built only from admiring other people's finished work is hollow. The studying matters, but the loop that compresses into judgment is decision, outcome, memory, repeated until you trust the instinct under pressure.
Does using AI to generate the options weaken your own taste over time?+
It can, if you let the tool make the call instead of just the candidates. The discipline is to use Gen AI for breadth, fifty starts instead of five, and to keep the choice firmly human, with a reason attached every time. Used that way it sharpens taste, because you make more decisions per week. Used lazily it atrophies the exact muscle the job now depends on.
If taste is the scarce skill, how does a junior creative break in?+
By getting accountable early, on something small and real, instead of building a reel of things nobody used. The fastest path is owning one channel or one product end to end, making the calls, and living with the results, because that is where judgment actually forms. The tools will hand a junior the same production power as a senior, so the differentiator is no longer who can make it, it is who has earned the judgment to choose.
