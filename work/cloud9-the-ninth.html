The situation

A fandom with no year-round home

Esports is shifting from one-off merch and tickets toward subscription digital ecosystems, the move the industry calls Membership 2.0, and the orgs that fold access and digital status into real tiers retain better. Cloud9 has more brand equity than almost any North American org and one of the few esports identities with genuine staying power, built on a calm, premium, fashion-credible register that is the deliberate opposite of aggressive gamer branding. What it has never built is a year-round home for that fandom.

Audience-first, the gap is obvious. A fan does not want another hoodie. They want status inside the in-group, real access to the players and the culture, and a reason to show up on a dead Tuesday when no match is live. The brand has the equity to carry a membership and has left the daily-belonging loop on the table.

Turn watching into belonging.

The Ninth is an additive membership sub-brand under Cloud9. The name comes from the three interwoven nines in the Cloud9 mark, and from cloud nine, the feeling of belonging the product sells. It runs on one idea: altitude. The higher you climb, the closer you get to cloud nine. And one rule holds the whole system together: the summit is earned, never sold. The top tier, Inner Nine, cannot be purchased anywhere in the product, which separates it from the pay-to-flex and NFT-pass models the category is full of, and fits Cloud9's calm register better than any bought status could.

02 · The product

An app built to earn a dead Tuesday

Community is owned inside the app, so tier roles create status where the org controls the surface. The member card is the live status object at the center.

03 · The AI, built

A working moment-alert clipper

Of four AI features, the clipper is the one prototyped and the one the product leads with. It serves the casual fans who do not know when to look up, and it is credible now: real clippers detect plays off kill feeds, objective captures, and audio or chat spikes. Three live signals fuse, the system catches the moment, and an auto-clip rises to the fan the instant it happens. The team still reviews before anything ships. Taste stays human.

05 · The social engine

One match in, a week out

The last layer closes the loop. One match produces a week of cross-platform output across a managed account portfolio, because that fan-out reaches far more fans than a single official account at the same cost. The clipper is the top of the funnel: each broadcast day yields 10 to 25 moments, the AI drafts platform-native variants per template, a human approves and tunes voice, and the set fans out on an event-driven cadence.

Outcome

What it demonstrates

One problem solved across brand, product, AI, motion, and social at once, with the decisions visible and defended. The recurring move is strategic restraint: free-to-play not gambling, earned not sold, calm not loud, owned community not outsourced, AI for volume but taste kept human. That restraint is the senior call in every layer, and it is the throughline a creative director is hired to hold.

Watching becomes belonging.
