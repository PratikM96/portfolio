Sports, Fans, and AI: The Membership Products the Next Decade Needs

The Ninth concept · SPORTIMESports · membership · AIMay 28, 20266 min read

I have built brands for real sports audiences and a full speculative membership system for an esports org. Across both, one shift keeps showing up: fandom is moving away from one-off merch and tickets toward year-round membership, and AI is about to change how fans watch and belong. The orgs that win the next decade will be the ones that design that shift on purpose instead of bolting AI onto a store.

The fan wants to belong on a Tuesday, not just on game day

Start with the person, as always. A real fan does not want another jersey. They want status inside the in-group, access to the culture, and a reason to engage when there is no match on. The category has historically sold them merch and a ticket and gone quiet in between. The opportunity is a product that gives the fandom a year-round home, with belonging and ritual at the center, not a checkout.

AI is the layer that makes every match personal

This is where the next decade gets interesting. A real-time AI layer can clip the moment something happens, tuned to the fan's team. It can act as a second commentator that knows who you root for. It can surface the moment to look up so a casual fan never misses the thing everyone will talk about. Done well, AI turns passive watching into a personal, participatory experience, which is exactly the daily-return loop a membership product needs.

Membership 2.0 is not perks bolted onto a store. It is a home for the fandom, with a real-time AI layer that makes the whole season feel made for you.

The hard part is tone, not technology

The technology is the easy half. The hard half is brand. Every org has a register, and the product has to feel like the org, not like a generic gamer dashboard or a casino. In the membership concept I built, the constraint that drove every decision was tone: calm, premium, and approachable, because that is who the org actually is. AI features that ignore the brand's character feel like a different company wandered into the app. The taste call is the moat.

Where this is heading

The same pattern extends well beyond one league. Women's sports media, fragmented and under-served, is a content-system and membership opportunity waiting for someone to build it audience-first. The through-line is consistent: design for what the fan actually wants, use AI to make the experience personal and the content scalable, and keep a human holding the brand's voice. The next great sports products will be membership systems with an AI heart and a very specific soul.
