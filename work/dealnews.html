00 / Situation

Seventeen million users, infrastructure built for a tenth of them

Seventeen million people showed up every month. The infrastructure was built for a fraction of them. Two years, three builds, one rule: if a decision did not move a business number, it did not ship.

DealNews aggregates deals for 17 million monthly users and ranks among the top 1,000 sites in the US. The audience was never the problem. The plumbing was. No self-serve ad platform, so every dollar of revenue ran through a sales rep. An app that had drifted away from the website it was supposed to mirror. Affiliate sites with no identity of their own. This was not a refresh. It was building the parts a company that size should already have had.

advertise.dealnews.com, the self-serve B2B marketplace, built from wireframe to launch.
01 / Marketplace · UX

Selling ad space should not require a salesperson

Seventeen million users and no way to buy a placement without booking a call. That is a revenue ceiling you can see from orbit. I designed the full self-serve marketplace end to end in Adobe XD, eight primary sections from homepage to a conversion-built application funnel, on a custom Shopify theme, plus the B2B social channels to feed inbound on day one. The bet was blunt: open the door to the small and mid-sized advertisers the sales team was never going to call.

Products page, one of eight primary sections designed across the platform.
Marketplace homepage scroll.
Result

Opened a revenue segment the sales team could not reach. Advertiser accounts roughly doubled, 200 to 400, in the first year.

02 / App redesign · UX

Every tap was leaking trust

The app had drifted. No third-party login, no sync with the website, thin filtering, icons that no longer matched the brand, and the store reviews said all of it out loud. So I rebuilt against each named complaint: Google, Apple, and Facebook sign-in, a real filter bar, brand-matched iconography, saved searches synced across platforms, and modal moments for tips and messages, carried through dev handoff to ship. Nothing on the page was decoration. Every change pointed at a friction someone had already written a one-star review about.

Every change pointed at a complaint.

Left: login with third-party sign-in, the new filter bar, and synced saved items. Right: the newsletter redesign across desktop and mobile.
Result

18 percent conversion lift after the redesign. Store ratings climbed to 4.6 and 4.7, up from 3.8 and 4.3.

03 / Sub-brand system · Brand

Three sub-brands that do not fight the parent

Three affiliate sites, Generator Tools, Refrigerator HQ, and Wake Up Sleepy Head, each had to stand on its own without snapping the thread back to DealNews. I built three logos with personality pulled from each product niche, held together by shared type and one structural logic, delivered across web, app icon, and avatar with guidelines for each. Distinct enough to live alone, related enough to read as family.

Three sub-brands, one network. Distinct personalities, shared structural logic.
04 / Graphics & marketing

The output nobody frames, and everybody needs

Under the three builds ran the daily work: sweepstakes graphics, seasonal campaigns, consumer survey infographics, newsletter redesigns for desktop and mobile, sales decks. The unglamorous proof that one system holds its consistency across a network of sites while shipping at volume.

Advertiser one-pagers and consumer survey infographics, part of an always-on output.
05 / Through-line

Four briefs, one system

An app measured in conversion and ratings. A marketplace that opened a segment sales could not reach. A sub-brand system that gave a network its own faces without cracking the parent. Product, brand, and marketing run as one connected practice, not three handoffs.
