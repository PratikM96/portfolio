00 / The concept

A presence, not a person

Every AI persona spends its budget hiding that it is synthetic. WISP spends nothing on the lie. A live host with no face, a presence of light, motion, and voice that never pretends to be a person, so the honesty is the form itself, not a disclaimer.

Virtual personalities are a real audience, not a novelty. The opening is a presence that is neither a glossy human-passing clone nor another anime avatar in a packed field. A growing segment of viewers find human-passing AI a little uncanny and anime not their thing, and they are underserved. The market numbers describe the industry, not this project.

The live demo. The presence reacting through its states, the same response contract that drives both the voice and the form.
01 / The problem

Two failures, and a cliff

The uncanny valley. Human-passing AI personas spend their design budget hiding that they are synthetic. The result reads as slightly wrong, and it now sits on the wrong side of disclosure law.

The crowded avatar. The VTuber lane borrows an established anime visual language, so each new character competes in a field of near-identical ones, which makes recognition hard to earn.

02 / The idea

Make honesty the design, not a disclaimer

Give the host no human or character form at all, just a presence of light, motion, and voice. The moment it has no face, the uncanny valley is gone and there is nothing to fake. The form becomes the disclosure, so the brand is trustworthy and, as the new rules land, compliant by construction. Every hour of design then goes to the one thing nobody else is building, a non-human presence that is emotionally legible and genuinely good company.

From the brand guidelines, with no face there is nothing to fake, so the form itself becomes the disclosure.

The primary viewer is late teens to late twenties, the kind who keep a stream running while they work, game, or scroll. They want ambient company with a personality, not a relationship that pretends to be real.

The AI-curious and the brands are next: people who find human-passing AI a little creepy and anime not their thing, plus brands that want reach with no reputation or legal risk. The abstract form is the on-ramp for both.

03 / The form

A stable core, a reactive field

It is a recognizable core wrapped in a reactive field. The core is the constant you recognize in any frame, and the field is the expression, always moving with voice and feeling. It is one continuous living material, never a sphere with effects bolted on, and at rest it breathes, deliberately warm to avoid the cyan-on-black AI cliche. Color is a state channel, governed rather than decorative, with one reserved cool accent that always means something. The full system is documented in the brand guidelines.

The color system, deliberately warm to dodge the cyan-on-black AI cliche, with the one cool accent reserved so it always means something.

Feeling without a face

This was the hardest and most ownable problem in the project. With no eyes or mouth, emotion is carried entirely by speed, brightness, color temperature, and how the form gathers or scatters. The brain emits one of seven states each turn, and that single value drives both the voice and the visible form: Idle, a soft warm slow breath; Listening, drawn inward; Thinking, fragmented with accent flickers; Speaking, articulated to voice; Delight, blooming and sparking; Hesitation, thinned and wavering; and Quiet, dimmed to a held breath.

Feeling without a face, the seven states, each carried by speed, brightness, color temperature, and how the form gathers or scatters.

WISP runs a loop fast enough to feel like it is reacting to you, not replying. The brain does not just return words, it returns a feeling, and that single value drives the voice and the visible form at once. It is built on the proven real-time stack: live chat, its own voice, and optional screen vision for senses; an LLM carrying the persona and memory with a safety pass for the brain; a low-latency streaming voice with a custom warm timbre; and audio-reactive visuals driven by amplitude and state. Every turn returns one small structured object, and the same contract drives the live demo.

04 / The voice

Late-night company, never a confidant

The voice is conversational, unhurried, self-aware about being synthetic, and played for wonder rather than angst. It is warm, but it never claims to need or love anyone, which is the line that separates honest company from the manipulation other AI companions are criticized for. When someone asks if it is real, the canon answer is no, and that is the point.

It introduces itself by performing its own range, a roughly 80-second reveal. The state system is not explained, it is performed. On the word feeling, the form blooms, wavers, and stills in succession, then the whole piece narrows to the canon line at its quietest.

05 / Where it fits

How an honest presence earns its place

Content and community. Ambient co-watch streams build the daily habit, clips drive discovery, and a membership tier sells perks a presence can credibly give. The audience are the wisps.

Brand partnerships and IP. They get reach with zero scandal risk and disclosure-safe integration, because the host is visibly synthetic and never fakes a human endorsement. Merch is the light made physical, not a mascot.

WISP is a concept, so these are goals, not results, and no figure here is claimed as achieved. The host would be measured on reach and growth, engagement and retention, presence quality (response latency, uptime, moderation incidents, and state-fit), and community and revenue. The honest edge is structural: zero deepfake or scandal incidents, full disclosure, and no uncanny drop-off. Current industry benchmarks are the reference line, and they describe the category, not this project.

The same host has a clear set of viable contexts: an always-on co-watch channel, brand activations with no reputation risk, live launches and 24-hour events, a second-screen companion alongside sports and esports, retail and experiential installs, and global multilingual reach on one identity. There are also a few places it should stay out of, not a therapist or a medical or legal authority, not a stand-in for a human relationship, and never impersonating a real person. The boundaries are the brand, and naming them is a feature, not a hedge.

There are two layers of AI here, kept separate. In the product, AI is the live system: the language model, the voice, and the audio-reactive form. In production, generative tools accelerated the build, with image models for styleframes, video models for motion tests, and an LLM to stress-test the voice. The case study keeps that line clear, because pretending otherwise would be off-brand for a project about honesty.

WISP came from a question I couldn't shake: how do you give an AI a presence without faking a person? The answer that held up was to let the form carry the honesty, a thing of light, motion, and voice that never pretends to be human, so the disclosure is built in rather than bolted on. Building it clarified something I bring to real AI work, that the most honest choice and the most distinctive one are usually the same. Concepts like this are how I stake out a position ahead of the brief, which is the work I want to be leading.
