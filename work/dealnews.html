DealNews · Social / Design / UX / Performance

From the advertiser pitch to the daily deal.

DealNews is one of the longest-running deals platforms on the web, with a large, established audience across its own brand and a network of affiliate sites. I was the lead in-house designer and ran the social accounts and paid advertising at the same time, so the creative and the performance never got handed to different people. The advertiser web, the app, three sub-brands, infographics, newsletters, decks, and the daily social, all from one desk.

2020 to 2022 · Social & Graphic Manager
The setup

Creative and performance, one desk

Most places split the people who make the work from the people who run it. At DealNews I was both, the lead in-house designer across the product and the marketing, and the one running social and paid. A feed post and the page it pointed at came from the same desk that watched how they performed, so the loop closed fast and the system stayed consistent across a whole network of sites.

01 / Advertiser web

Designing the B2B side

DealNews sells to advertisers as well as shoppers, so I designed the advertiser-facing side at advertise.dealnews.com in Adobe XD on a custom Shopify build, from the homepage through the application flow, and ran the B2B social alongside it. The job was to make the advertiser side legible and self-explanatory for the small and mid-sized buyers the brand wanted.

02 / App UI

Fixing what the reviews flagged

The mobile app had drifted from the site and the brand. I designed the updates against the complaints showing up in store reviews: third-party sign-in, a real filter bar, brand-matched icons, and synced saved searches, carried through to dev handoff. Nothing decorative, every change pointed at a friction someone had already written a review about.

From sign-in to the new filter bar and synced saves.
03 / Sub-brands

Three sites, one family

Three affiliate sites in the network, Generator Tools, Refrigerator HQ, and Wake Up Sleepy Head, each needed to stand on its own without snapping the thread back to DealNews. I built three logos with personality pulled from each niche, held together by shared type and one structural logic, delivered across web, app icon, and avatar. Distinct enough to live alone, related enough to read as family.

Three sub-brands, one network, distinct personalities on a shared structural logic.
04 / The daily

The volume nobody frames

Under the bigger builds ran the daily work that keeps a deals brand alive: consumer-survey infographics, the desktop and mobile newsletters, advertiser one-pagers and sales decks, and the social that carried all of it. The unglamorous proof that one system holds its look across a network while shipping at volume.

Context

DealNews has run independently since 1997 and pulls more than 250 million visits a year across its network, the scale the work had to hold up across.

What it taught me

This was the role that set the pattern for everything after. Designing the work and running the campaigns at the same desk taught me that the creative and the numbers are not two jobs to hand between two people, they are one loop, and the loop closes fastest when one person owns both ends. I have run the creative and the channels as one practice ever since.
