A full-scale rebrand and content program across the largest tennis club network in the Northeast. Three sub-brands, 15+ accounts, and a $55M facility expansion, all running simultaneously.

Brand board: SPORTIME Clubs, JMTA, SPORTIME Pickleball, and sub-brand marks, built as one coherent system.

The Situation

SPORTIME operates one of the largest tennis and racquet sport networks in the Northeast. By early 2023 the brand had three distinct product verticals, tennis clubs, a new pickleball division, and the John McEnroe Tennis Academy, each needing its own identity while staying coherent within the parent system.

The social presence was stagnant. Accounts across 15+ locations were gaining minimal followers, posting inconsistent content, and generating low engagement relative to the size of the network. At the same time, a $55M facility expansion across two JMTA campuses needed a complete marketing program ready before opening day.

One person. All of it.

The Work

Brand System

Built a complete logo production system covering five brand marks: SPORTIME Clubs, JMTA, SPORTIME Pickleball, Volleyball, and individual identities for all club locations. Each delivered in full source AI files with exports across every required format and colorway, plus a brand guideline template covering usage, spacing, and approved applications.

SPORTIME brand header motion.
JMTA brand film, produced for the academy launch.

SPORTIME Tennis / Social and Content Rebuild

Rebuilt the content strategy across all 13 club locations from the ground up. A video-first approach replaced static-heavy feeds. A unified marketing calendar coordinated output across every location. Event photography and a templated collateral system gave staff the tools to execute locally without the output going off-brand.

Result

From a near-zero posting baseline to 7,354 Instagram posts in 2023, including 3,115 Reels. On Facebook, organic impressions across all club pages rose 83% year over year to 2.7M, with video output up 783%.

Play Tennis 101 and Road to NYC campaign sets, plus HEAD Speed and Wilson Blade equipment-partner content.

JMTA / John McEnroe Tennis Academy

The $55M expansion across Long Island and New York City campuses added courts, pickleball facilities, and a Community Welcome Center, ultimately making SPORTIME Randall's Island the largest indoor tennis training facility in the world at completion. All of that new capacity needed marketing materials ready before opening day, and enrollment momentum from day one.

Produced a full suite of print and digital assets across both campuses: event graphics, Team Cup promotional materials, seasonal campaign visuals, and co-branded SPORTIME and JMTA collateral, delivered simultaneously across print vendors, social platforms, and internal communications.

Result

All materials delivered ahead of opening. JMTA became the highest-reach account in the network within the year, the largest Instagram account at 15,154 followers (+3,131 for the year) and the top page on both platforms by organic impressions. The new facility reached approximately 65% court utilization within 90 days of opening.

Combine event campaign and scholarship and tryouts collateral across both campuses.

SPORTIME Pickleball / Brand Identity and Launch

Pickleball grew 30% nationally in 2022 and was named the fastest-growing sport in America for the third consecutive year in 2023. SPORTIME needed a brand for its dedicated vertical that could stand alone, scale across 200+ courts, and support tournaments, clinics, and co-branded programs without requiring a designer at every location.

Developed a modernized logo system with cleaner typography and a refreshed badge format. Built a scalable template library covering tournament flyers, gift cards, open-play promotions, and event series graphics. Motion trailers and compilation reels produced to support launch and ongoing promotion.

Result

Vertical launched across 200+ Northeast courts with a full brand and content system in place. Membership inquiries for pickleball-specific programming increased approximately 20% in the six months following the rebrand, supported by a credible brand identity that competitors in the region had not established yet.
