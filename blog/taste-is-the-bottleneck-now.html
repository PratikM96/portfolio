Taste Is the Bottleneck Now

Creative directionJudgment · the CD roleMay 19, 20265 min read

For most of the history of this field, the constraint was production. Making the thing was hard, slow, and expensive, so the people who could make it well were valuable for that. The tools have quietly removed that constraint. When anything can be generated in seconds, the bottleneck moves to the one thing that was never about production: knowing which of the thousand possible things is the right one.

Making was the moat, and the moat is draining

Be honest about what is happening. A large part of what creative work used to charge for was execution, the hours and the craft of producing a polished asset. AI is collapsing that cost toward zero. That is genuinely destabilizing if execution was your whole value. It is liberating if it was not, because the part that is getting expensive, relatively, is judgment, and judgment was always the harder thing.

What a director actually does when everything is cheap

When I can generate fifty directions in an afternoon, my job is no longer to make one. It is to throw away forty-nine for reasons I can defend. To know that this one will land with a tired person at the end of their day and that one will not. To hold a coherent intent across every surface so the fifty pieces feel like one thing. The scarce act is selection, direction, and restraint, and none of those come from a prompt.

The tools can make anything. They cannot tell you what is worth making. That gap is the whole job now.

Taste is not vibes, it is compressed experience

Taste sounds soft, so people discount it. It is not soft. It is pattern recognition built from years of seeing what works, for whom, and why, then applied fast and under pressure. A model has read everything and decided nothing. A director has decided a thousand times and remembers what happened. That accumulated judgment is exactly the thing that does not transfer into a model, because it lives in having been accountable for outcomes.

The reassuring part, and the demand it makes

This is good news for anyone who got into this to think, not just to render. The role gets more strategic and less about labor, which is where creative direction should have been all along. But it raises the bar. If execution is no longer where you add value, your judgment has to be genuinely good, and you have to be able to say why one option beats another in terms a client and an engineer both understand. Taste becomes the deliverable. That is a harder, better job.
