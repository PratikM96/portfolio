The situation

Early career, serious clients

Agency FiveEighty is a New York creative agency working across broadcast, digital, and brand. My work here ran across motion graphics, brand identity, competitive display advertising, and event photography for the agency's clients, including FISLL, the New York Jets, Spectrum, and Coca-Cola. Early career, but the client list and production standards were not.

01 / FISLL

Motion and photography

FISLL is a values-driven athletic brand producing consistent video content with no brand bookends. Every piece went out without a visual identity at open or close.

Produced two sequences in After Effects. The outro: a fire-based logo reveal built on FISLL's circular emblem, energetic without abandoning the mark. The intro: a gritty street-level sequence combining bold animated typography with live-action footage. Both built to work at full length and cut down for social without re-editing. Also produced five Instagram video posts and full event photography, 78 edited final images from a single shoot.

03 / Spectrum

One ad per rival

Produced a full competitive display campaign for Spectrum targeting named competitors in regional markets, 33 ads total across desktop, mobile, and tablet. Each ad built against a specific competitor: ATT Gig, Google Fiber, Frontier Fios, Buckeye, Giggle, Ashland, Allo, and others. Designed to intercept audiences actively comparing internet service providers in markets where Spectrum and those competitors were head to head.

What it taught me

FiveEighty set the production standard for everything that followed. Building broadcast-ready motion and a 33-ad conquest system taught me to design the master so it flexes, never shooting for one format and retrofitting the rest. That front-loaded, multi-format thinking is most of what makes a fast turnaround possible without the speed showing. Internalizing an agency's bar early is what gave me the range to lead production across channels instead of specializing into one.
