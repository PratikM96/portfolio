00 / Situation

I measure a role by what runs without me after I leave. Four years growing a volunteer design division from 5 to 15+ creatives for a global humanitarian nonprofit, then the real deliverable: handing it off as its own function.

No dedicated design team

The US division had no dedicated design team. Every creative request ran through the broader marketing function, creating a bottleneck that slowed output for an organization running an expanding portfolio of global humanitarian initiatives across food relief, healthcare, and education.

Brand outro animation, part of the bookend system built for the org's video content.
01 / The work

A team built to run without me

Founded a standalone volunteer design division from scratch and grew it from 5 to 15+ creatives over four years. Established the workflows, briefing processes, and quality standards that let the team handle every collateral request independently from marketing, and mentored the junior designers as it grew. The goal from the start was a team that could run without me.

Deliverables produced during this period include brand animation sequences, social media graphics, brochures, posters, newsletters, and video editing for recap and testimonial content.

04 / Through-line

The handoff

By 2018 the team had grown large enough to formally separate from the marketing division as its own dedicated creative function, built on infrastructure designed to sustain long-term output independently.

The brief was never really the brochures. It was building a creative function that could run without me, and growing it from five volunteers to fifteen-plus meant designing workflows, standards, and people, not just artwork. Learning to scale a team and hand off my own judgment is the part of this work that reads as leadership, not craft. It's the experience I lean on most when I make the case for running a department, not just a project.
