The situation

Hard to explain, easy to ignore

Forest Road works in film tax credits and lending, products that are hard to explain and harder to make compelling to institutional investors. They had no content infrastructure to tell that story, or the business behind it, across digital channels. The brief was legibility, under the eyes of people who would not forgive a simplified version.

01 / The work

A company history that respects the room

The centerpiece was a flagship company-history film built in After Effects, from founding to the present, designed to make dense financial content readable without sanding off what makes it credible.

Around it sat an editorial calendar for LinkedIn and investor channels, and an executive analytics layer tracking CAC, engagement, and ROI, the reporting the firm had been operating without.

Supporting motion, built for the firm's social and onboarding channels.
The result

Content turned into infrastructure

The film became a core asset across investor pitches, onboarding, and social. The tracking gave leadership a consistent read on what the content was doing and what it cost, turning one-off content into something the firm could keep using and measuring.

What it taught me

Institutional investors will not forgive a simplified story or sit through a dense one. Landing the company history meant respecting how much the room already knew, then building the measurement so leadership could see what the work was doing. Making the thing and proving its value is what earns a creative lead a place in the business conversation instead of a brief handed down to them. That is the job I am moving toward.
