The situation

Davos in the Desert

The Future Investment Initiative is Saudi Arabia's flagship global investment forum, the gathering the press nicknamed "Davos in the Desert." Richard Attias and Associates, the agency behind it, builds these events end to end. For the 2019 third edition, held at the Ritz-Carlton and King Abdulaziz Conference Center in Riyadh from October 29 to 31 under the theme "What is Next for Global Business," the forum had to read as world-class the moment a head of state, a sovereign-fund chief, or a global broadcaster walked in.

That meant one coherent visual system running across every surface at once: the physical hall, the screens, the printed program, the signage, the app, and the broadcast feed. At this scale, a forum is a brand experience measured in square meters and camera angles, not just pages.

01 / The visual system

Twelve themes, one grid

As Associate Design Manager, I worked on environmental, digital, and print creative across the edition, all holding to one bilingual identity in English and Arabic, a dark, premium base with a luminous teal-to-green network motif that ran from the printed factsheet to the screens to the hall.

The 2019 edition was organized around three pillars, three summits, and twelve cross-sector taskforces, each with a one-word codename. Twelve themes, twelve icons, one grid. Designing that many distinct ideas into a system that still reads as one forum is the core challenge of event identity at this scale.

The constellation identity, program architecture, and the twelve taskforces, one grid holding all of it.
Across every surface

UX for physical space

The forum also launched the FII Institute during the 2019 edition, and the same identity had to carry the institutional story: PIF's Vision 2030 context, the Institute's four objectives, the program's place in the Kingdom's economic transformation, dense, high-stakes content that had to stay readable and credible for an audience of heads of state, sovereign-fund chiefs, and global press.

The system extended across every surface of the forum: stage backgrounds and main-hall scenic built to broadcast safe zones, large-format installs and bilingual wayfinding that moved 6,000 people through the venue, digital signage and session displays, social and web content for the live program, and the full suite of printed delegate materials. Environmental design is UX for physical space, and at FII the print, the screens, and the hall all had to speak the same visual language at once.

The result

Coherent enough to disappear

Every surface delivered on schedule, on-brand, and on-camera. The 2019 edition was one of the most visible business gatherings in the world that year, and the creative had to be invisible in the right way: so coherent that the system, not any single asset, was what people remembered.

Sub-project / Global AI Summit

Two arguments about AI

Alongside the FII work, I explored the identity for the Global AI Summit, a related forum positioning Saudi Arabia in the artificial-intelligence conversation. I developed a bilingual brand system, Futura for English and Montserrat Arabic for Arabic, with two distinct logo directions built around the "Ai" letterform.

The first renders the "A" as a low-poly, faceted form in a warm orange-to-red gradient, machine-built and data-like. The second reads the "Ai" as a figure, an abstract person mid-stride in a cool green-to-teal gradient, putting the human at the center of the AI story. Two directions, two arguments about what the summit really was.

What it taught me

FII is the headline, but the real education at RAA was the standard itself. The agency builds these forums end to end, and my time there ran across several, FII, Bloomberg's New Economy Forum, and the RFP and pitch work that wins the next one. Everything had to read as world-class the moment a head of state walked in, often in two languages, across the hall, the screens, the print, and the broadcast. Operating at that level of stakes early set the bar I now hold my own creative leadership to.
