The situation

Early in, deep end

Agency FiveEighty is a New York agency working across broadcast, digital, and brand. My work ran motion graphics, brand identity, competitive display, and event photography for its roster, FISLL, the New York Jets, Spectrum, and Coca-Cola. The career was early. The client list and the production standard were not. For scale context, the Jets work reached into a 1.7M-fan audience and the Spectrum campaign ran across a 31M-customer footprint, the size of the rooms, not numbers I drove.

01 / FISLL

Bookends, built to cut down

FISLL is a values-driven athletic brand shipping steady video with no brand bookends, every piece going out naked at open and close.

I built two sequences in After Effects: a fire-based logo reveal on FISLL's circular emblem, energetic without losing the mark, and a gritty street-level intro marrying bold animated type to live action. Both were built to run full length and to cut down for social without a re-edit. Plus five Instagram videos and full event photography, 78 edited finals from a single shoot.

03 / Spectrum

One ad per rival

A full competitive display campaign for Spectrum against named competitors in regional markets, 33 ads across desktop, mobile, and tablet. Each one built against a specific rival, ATT Gig, Google Fiber, Frontier Fios, Buckeye, and others, to intercept audiences actively comparing providers in markets where Spectrum and that rival were head to head. Eleven competitors, three formats each, one master system that flexed to all of it.

What it taught me

FiveEighty set the production standard for everything after it. Building broadcast-ready motion and a 33-ad conquest system taught me to design the master so it flexes, never shooting for one format and retrofitting the rest. That front-loaded, multi-format thinking is most of what makes a fast turnaround possible without the speed showing. Internalizing an agency's bar this early is what gave me the range to lead production across channels instead of specializing into one.
