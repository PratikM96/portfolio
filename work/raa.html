The situation

Davos in the Desert

The Future Investment Initiative is Saudi Arabia's flagship global investment forum. Richard Attias and Associates builds it end to end. For the 2019 edition at the Ritz-Carlton and King Abdulaziz Conference Center, under the theme of what is next for global business, one visual system had to run across every surface at once: the hall, the screens, the printed program, the signage, the app, and the broadcast feed.

At this scale a forum is a brand experience measured in square meters and camera angles, not pages. Hold one identity coherent across all of it, bilingual in English and Arabic, in front of heads of state, and there is no acceptable version but the final one.

01 / The visual system

Twelve themes, one grid

As Associate Design Manager I worked the environmental, digital, and print creative across the edition, all of it holding to one bilingual identity, a dark, premium base with a luminous teal-to-green network motif running from the printed factsheet to the screens to the hall.

The 2019 program was organized around three pillars, three summits, and twelve cross-sector taskforces, each with a one-word codename. Twelve themes, twelve icons, one grid. Designing that many distinct ideas into a system that still reads as one forum is the whole job at this level.

The constellation identity, program architecture, and the twelve taskforces, one grid holding all of it.
Across every surface

Environmental design is UX for physical space

The same identity carried the launch of the FII Institute during the edition, dense institutional content, Vision 2030 context, the Institute's objectives, that had to stay readable and credible for heads of state, sovereign-fund chiefs, and global press.

It ran across stage and main-hall scenic built to broadcast-safe zones, large-format installs and bilingual wayfinding that moved 6,000 people through the venue, digital signage, session displays, and the full suite of printed delegate materials. Print, screen, and hall, all speaking one language at once.

The result

Coherent enough to disappear

Every surface delivered on schedule, on brand, and on camera. The 2019 edition was one of the most visible business gatherings in the world that year, and the creative had to be invisible in the right way: so coherent that the system, not any single asset, was what people remembered.

Sub-project / Global AI Summit

Two arguments about AI, one brief

Alongside FII I explored the identity for the Global AI Summit, a related forum positioning the Kingdom in the AI conversation. I built a bilingual system, Futura for English and Montserrat Arabic for Arabic, with two logo directions on the "Ai" letterform.

The first reads the "A" as a faceted, machine-built form in a warm orange-to-red gradient, data made visible. The second reads the "Ai" as a figure mid-stride in a cool green-to-teal, the human at the center of the AI story. Two directions, two arguments about what the summit really was.

Two directions from one brief, and the brand spec: palette, typography, and usage in one sheet.
The AI Summit brand spec: dual color systems and bilingual typography, English in Futura, Arabic in Montserrat Arabic.
What it taught me

FII is the headline, but the real education was the standard. The agency builds these forums end to end, and my time there ran across several, FII, Bloomberg's New Economy Forum, and the pitch work that wins the next one. Everything had to read world-class the moment a head of state walked in, often in two languages, across the hall, the screens, the print, and the broadcast. Operating at that level of stakes early set the bar I now hold my own creative leadership to.
