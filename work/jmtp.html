Johnny Mac Tennis Project · Brand / Social / Event / Photography

A-list nights, for the kids' game.

The Johnny Mac Tennis Project is John McEnroe's nonprofit, putting tennis in the hands of kids across Harlem and the Bronx. I ran the brand, social, and event photography for its two 2023 fundraisers, a Hamptons Pro-Am and a Comedy Night. The creative had to look as A-list as the room and help each night do the one thing it came to do, raise money.

2023 · Marketing Manager, SPORTIME
The brief

Look the part, move the money

Two events, one job: make a youth-tennis charity feel like the marquee night it actually was, across the run-up on the feed, the room itself, and the photography that carries it into next year. The work is judged by a crowd that notices when the craft slips, and by whether the wallets open.

01 / The Pro-Am

A Hamptons Pro-Am, 128 players deep

The Pro-Am is the project's biggest day, a charity tournament at SPORTIME Amagansett that pairs amateurs with tennis legends. I built the social run-up and shot the day itself, the coverage that makes next year's ticket sell itself.

The Pro-Am social campaign, built for the run-up to the day.

Shot courtside through the day at SPORTIME Amagansett.

The event

The 2023 Pro-Am raised a record $650,000 in a single day, besting the prior $600,000, with 128 pro and amateur players including John and Patrick McEnroe, Jim Courier, and James Blake.

02 / Comedy Night

A night at Sony Hall

The off-season fundraiser trades the court for a comedy stage. I ran the brand and social for the night and covered it on the floor, the same project in a completely different room.

The Comedy Night campaign, cut for feed and story.

The event

The Comedy Night fundraiser at Sony Hall, hosted by John and Patrick McEnroe, with a comedy lineup including Tracy Morgan and Mike Birbiglia.

What it taught me

The point was never the poster. It was the kids the night was raising money for. The job was to give a charity event the polish of a marquee brand without letting it lose the warmth of a cause, fast, under pressure, in front of a room that notices when the craft slips. The best version of this work disappears, and all anyone remembers is that the night felt worth being at and worth giving to.
