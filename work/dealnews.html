The Situation

DealNews is a deal-aggregation platform trusted by 17 million monthly users and ranked among the top 1,000 websites in the US. When I joined, the infrastructure had not kept pace with the audience: no self-serve ad platform, an app that had drifted from the website experience, and affiliate properties with no individual brand identities.

The Work

Marketplace / UX

DealNews had 17M monthly users and no way for advertisers to buy placements without going through a sales rep. That created a hard ceiling on revenue and locked out the small to mid-sized brand segment entirely.

Designed the full UI/UX framework in Adobe XD across all primary pages and sub-flows: Homepage, About, Products, Process, Clients, Learning Center, Application Form, and Thank You. Built on Shopify with a custom theme, a conversion-optimized layout, and a streamlined advertiser application funnel. Launched dedicated B2B social accounts to support inbound at launch.

App Redesign / UX

The app had measurable friction. Login had no third-party sign-in support, there was no account sync between app and website, filtering was limited, and the iconography had diverged from the brand. App store reviews reflected all of it, with users calling out bloated navigation, missing features, and inconsistent branding.

Rebuilt the login screen with Google, Apple, and Facebook sign-in. Added a new filter bar, updated iconography aligned to the website, synced saved searches across platforms, and introduced modal interactions for marketing messages and tips. Every change addressed a specific friction point, executed in collaboration with the dev team on handoff and implementation.

Sub-brand Identity System / Brand

Three DealNews affiliate sites, Generator Tools, Refrigerator HQ, and Wake Up Sleepy Head, each needed an individual identity while remaining recognizably connected to the parent network. Developed three distinct logos, each with unique iconography and personality reflecting its product niche, unified through shared typeface choices and structural logic. Delivered across web, app icon, and social avatar formats with brand guideline documentation for each.

Three sub-brands, one network. Distinct personalities, shared structural logic.

Maintained visual consistency across a network of sites while supporting day-to-day marketing output at volume.

What it taught me

DealNews was four briefs under one logo: an app, a marketplace, three sub-brands, and a marketing calendar. The lesson that stuck is that none of them moved until I stopped treating them as separate problems and ran them as one system. That shift, from executing deliverables to owning how the parts connect, is the jump from senior designer to creative direction. It's how I've worked on every engagement since, and the capability I'm building a leadership role around.
