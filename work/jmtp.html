Pro Am event banner, built around the Hamptons setting.

The Situation

The Johnny Mac Tennis Project provides access to tennis and opportunities for personal growth to students from under-resourced communities in East Harlem and the South Bronx. Their annual fundraising events, the Pro Am in the Hamptons and the Comedy Night in New York City, are high-profile occasions attended by tennis legends, donors, and press. The 2023 Pro Am featured John and Patrick McEnroe, Jim Courier, James Blake, Mats Wilander, and Christina McHale among 128 players. The materials needed to match that caliber.

The Work

Two events. Two very different visual briefs.

The Pro Am got illustrated event banner graphics built around the Hamptons setting, capturing the venue and occasion without being generic. The Comedy Night at a New York theater was photographed live, with edited assets delivered in organized, media-ready packages structured for social, editorial, and donor-facing use.

Every asset delivered on schedule. No missed deadlines, no format gaps.

Pro Am campaign system

Beyond the headline banner, the Pro Am ran a full promotional sequence: ticket and feed graphics, an About and Sponsors carousel, a countdown series, and an event-day post.

Comedy Night social set

The Comedy Night carried its own social set across square posts and stories, built from the live theater photography.

Result

The 2023 Pro Am broke the event's fundraising record, surpassing the previous $600,000, with attendance in the range of 450 to 600 players, donors, and guests.

What it taught me

Two fundraisers, two completely different rooms, no second takes. The Pro Am wanted polish and the Hamptons; Comedy Night wanted energy and timing, and shooting both back to back taught me to read an occasion before imposing a look on it. Live work doesn't reward your favorite style, it rewards the one the moment actually needs. Learning to subordinate taste to the brief is a discipline I carry into every room I now art-direct.
