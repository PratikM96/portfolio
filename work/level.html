The opening

A booming category, racing into a harm problem.

Prediction markets exploded in 2026, and the field is sprinting toward a trillion-dollar future while a documented youth-gambling problem builds underneath it. Sportsbooks are flooding in, optimizing for engagement, and the people most at risk are the easiest to reach. Level is the responsible one, built to still be the one people actually want: plain-language odds, an explainer in front of every market, and protection on by default, in the corner of the category where it matters most.

The approach

Lead with responsibility, in the corner where it is hardest.

The easy read was wrong. The premise that every rival looks like crypto-gambling no longer holds, the market leader is already clean. So Level does not win on calmer visuals. It wins on a designed system of protection and legibility, and it plants that flag in sports, the most lucrative and most harmful corner, where the rest of the field is gamifying hardest.

Responsible by default

Friction that protects, not exploits. Limits on by default, an honest Starter mode for the 18 to 20 cohort, no engagement bait.

Legible by default

Plain-language odds and an assistant that explains a market before you stake. You cannot decide responsibly about something you do not understand.

Built to grow up

Sports now, the wider world later. The whole system is category-agnostic, so it extends into economics and politics without a relaunch.

Who it is for

Design for the curious, protect the at-risk.

The curious fan

Follows the game, wants to back a read, does not want a terminal or a slot machine. Served by legibility.

The at-risk user

The young cohort reachable a rung below the sportsbook wall. Served by protection, on by default.

The trust halo

Parents, regulators, the press. They never stake, but they decide whether Level reads as the credible one.

The differentiator

Protection, designed as a system.

The responsible-use layer is not a disclaimer, it is the product, designed first and built into every screen. It is also the senior-judgment proof: the part that shows restraint and a point of view, not just craft.

Friction that protects

Limits on by default, raising one takes a day, lowering is instant. Cooldowns after a losing run. An understand-before-you-stake gate on unfamiliar markets.

The inverse of an engagement model

The same behavioral model the category uses to maximize time-on-app, pointed the other way: it watches for problem patterns and intervenes with a check-in and resources, never an upsell.

Honest by construction

Fees and the house position shown up front. The real net, not just the potential win, is the number on the stake screen. No streaks, no urgency theater.

Designed to be used

Self-exclusion is one tap, with real cooling-off options and no friction to leave. Red is reserved for stop, never for an ordinary loss.

Why the gate matters

Legibility as harm reduction, made interactive. In a category that hides the odds behind a slick interface, Level puts a teacher in front of the stake. You cannot stake on a market you have not understood, which is the whole concept in one interaction.

  • Plain language, never jargon or hype.
  • Names the risk honestly, never sells a sure thing.
  • Shows the evidence, with the fee up front.

Interactive, in Level's voice. Every market, every first-time user.
