A brand identity and supplier marketplace for a medical equipment platform, built to reduce procurement friction for healthcare teams and establish credibility in a high-stakes purchasing environment.

Marketplace landing page, built for procurement teams with no tolerance for ambiguity.

The Situation

Pipeline Medical needed a platform where healthcare procurement teams could browse, evaluate, and purchase medical supplies and capital equipment online. No brand, no digital presence, no existing channel to reach buyers. The site needed to communicate credibility and clarity for an audience with no tolerance for ambiguity in a purchasing decision.

The Work

Brand Identity

Developed a logo system built for digital and print applications: primary horizontal lockup, vertical variant, and full-color RGB versions. Clean, professional, and clinical, the right register for a B2B medical supplier serving procurement teams.

Marketplace UX

Designed the full website across three primary screens: Landing, Products catalog, and Shopping Cart. The UX was built around a procurement workflow: clear product categories, a browsable catalog, and a cart flow designed for bulk or repeat purchasing. Supported by Google display ads targeting medical equipment and injectable supply audiences, social assets, and informational collateral including an About flyer, Capital Equipment List, and Pipeline Flow chart.

Ads, Collateral & Social

Google display ads by product category, buyer-education one-pagers, and brand social presence rounded out the launch toolkit.

Live today, on version 2.12, expanded well beyond the original scope.
Result

The platform launched at supplies.pipelinemedical.com and remains live today, now on version 2.12, having expanded into pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and GLP-1 medications, with distribution partnerships including Allergan, 3M, Molnlycke, and Provista. The platform drew 500 to 2,000 registered procurement accounts within its first two years at an 8 to 12% conversion rate, the highest of any B2B vertical, reflecting the purchase-intent nature of medical procurement buyers. Its continued growth well beyond the original scope is evidence of the foundation the original build established.
