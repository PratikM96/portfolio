The situation

Hard to explain, easy to ignore

Forest Road works in film tax credits and lending, products that are hard to explain and harder to make compelling to institutional investors. They had closed a $28M Series B and had no content infrastructure to tell that story, or the business behind it, across digital channels. The brief was legibility, under the eyes of people who would not forgive a simplified version.

01 / The work

A company history that respects the room

The centerpiece was a flagship company-history film built in After Effects, founding through the Series B, designed to make dense financial content readable without sanding off what makes it credible.

Around it sat an editorial calendar for LinkedIn and investor channels, and an executive analytics layer tracking CAC, engagement, and ROI, the reporting the firm had been operating without.

Supporting motion, built for the firm's social and onboarding channels.
The result

A milestone turned into infrastructure

The film became a core asset across investor pitches, onboarding, and social. The analytics gave leadership a consistent read on what the content was doing and what it cost, turning a one-time fundraising milestone into something the firm could keep using. The 4 percent engagement on native B2B finance video ran against a 5.1 percent platform benchmark, on some of the least snackable content there is, which for this audience is a strong read.

What it taught me

Institutional investors will not forgive a simplified story or sit through a dense one. Landing the company history meant respecting how much the room already knew, then building the measurement so leadership could see what the work was doing. Pairing craft with proof, making the thing and showing its value, is what earns a creative lead a seat in the business conversation rather than a brief handed down to it. That is the version of the job I am moving toward.
