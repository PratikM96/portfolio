The situation

Early in, deep end

Agency FiveEighty is a New York agency working across broadcast, digital, and brand. My work ran motion graphics, brand identity, competitive display, and event photography for its roster, FISLL, the New York Jets, Spectrum, and Coca-Cola. I was early. The client list and the production standard were not.

01 / FISLL

Bookends, built to cut down

FISLL is a values-driven athletic brand that was shipping steady video with no brand bookends, every piece going out naked at the open and the close. I built both ends in After Effects, a fire-based reveal on the circular emblem and a gritty street-level intro marrying bold animated type to live action, each built to run full length and cut down for social without a re-edit. I also shot the event and delivered 78 edited finals from the one shoot.

03 / Spectrum

One ad per rival

A full competitive display campaign for Spectrum against named competitors in regional markets, across desktop, mobile, and tablet. Each unit was built against a specific rival, ATT Gig, Google Fiber, Frontier Fios, Buckeye, and others, to intercept audiences actively comparing providers in markets where Spectrum and that rival were head to head. One ad per named competitor, three formats each, one master system that flexed to all of it.

What it taught me

FiveEighty set the production standard for everything after it. Building broadcast-ready motion and a competitive conquest system taught me to front-load the system, designing one asset that scales to every format instead of cutting for one and patching the rest in later. That multi-format thinking is most of what makes a fast turnaround possible without the speed showing. Internalizing an agency's bar this early is what gave me the range to lead production across channels instead of specializing into one.
