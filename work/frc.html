The situation

Hard to explain, easy to ignore

The Forest Road Company operates in film tax credits and lending, products that are hard to explain and harder to make compelling to institutional investors and B2B partners. They had closed a $28M Series B round and had no content infrastructure to communicate that story, or the broader business behind it, across digital channels.

01 / The work

A company history, in motion

The centerpiece was a flagship motion-graphic company-history piece built in After Effects, documenting key milestones from founding through the Series B. Designed to make dense financial content accessible without simplifying it, with the clarity and visual credibility that a B2B investor audience expects.

Alongside it: a full editorial content calendar for LinkedIn and investor-channel distribution, and executive-facing analytics frameworks tracking CAC, engagement, and ROI, giving leadership the reporting infrastructure the firm had been operating without.

Supporting motion, built for the firm's social and onboarding channels.
The result

Milestone to infrastructure

The history piece became a core asset used across investor pitches, onboarding, and social distribution. The analytics frameworks gave leadership a consistent view of content performance and cost that had not existed before, turning a one-time fundraising milestone into reusable infrastructure.

What it taught me

Forest Road was a lesson in legibility under pressure: institutional investors won't forgive a simplified story or sit through a dense one. Landing the company history meant respecting how much the audience already knew, then building the analytics layer so leadership could see what the content was doing. Pairing craft with measurement, making the work and proving its value, is what lets a creative lead earn a seat in the business conversation. That combination is the version of myself I'm advancing toward.
